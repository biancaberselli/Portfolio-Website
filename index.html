What I study

Electrical Engineering

As an Electrical Engineering student at the University of Calgary, I am provided with countless opportunities to apply theoretical knowledge into hands-on practice.

I have gained experience with working with digital circuits through MIPS assembly language and learning about Central Processing Units (CPUs). I have worked with the PIC18F27/47/57Q43 microcontroller in order to combine software and hardware functions.

I have built circuits to study the behaviors of various electronic devices and materials such as diodes, rectifiers and regulators, transistors, and optoelectronics.

Using the Intel Quartus program, I have designed digital circuits to complete various desired functions.

In my post-secondary studies I have aquired technical skills such as the C, Python, HTML, CSS programming languages, MATLAB, and am currently learning how to analyze circuits using MATLAB.

Biomedical Engineering

With the addition of a minor in Biomedical Engineering, I am learning how to implement the knowledge and practical skills learned in Electrical Engineering, in the Biomedical field. In the courses taken thus far, I have been introduced to biomechanics, instrumentation and devices, physiological and biological measurements.

The minor has introduced me to the current technology utilized in the medical field, and how each engineering discipline is essential in the health care system. I am currently studying the innovative process to find solutions to current unsolved issues in the medical field.
